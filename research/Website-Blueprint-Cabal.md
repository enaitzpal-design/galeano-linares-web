# Blueprint: The Winning Formula for Cabal's Website
### Evidence-based research into fontanería/calefacción businesses in Spain

**Prepared for:** Cabal (Félix Galeano & Ramón Linares) — fontanería y calefacción, Mataró
**Purpose:** A blueprint that can be handed to another model/team to build the website, grounded in what measurably works and fails for comparable Spanish businesses.
**Status:** Final — incorporates an independent cross-validation pass (see Section 6).

---

## 0. Method

Two independent research passes (via FireCrawl scraping + web search, conducted 2026-07-13):

- **Pass A — 10 successful businesses**: selected for strong Google reputation (mostly 4.6–5.0★, 47–1400+ reviews across multiple independent sources) *and* a real, live, purpose-built website. Full homepage structure was scraped and documented section-by-section.
- **Pass B — 10 struggling businesses**: selected for verifiable negative signals — Trustpilot TrustScores as low as 1.0–2.8/5, formal OCU (Organización de Consumidores y Usuarios) consumer complaints, dead/broken sites, doorway-page networks, or directory-only presence with no working website. Two of the ten (Servicio Xpress, Servis Instal Company) operate live "fontanero-mataro" pages — direct local competitors to Cabal.

Every rating, quote and structural claim below is sourced in the two full research reports (available on request); this document distills them into decision-usable form. Where evidence was inconclusive, that is stated rather than smoothed over.

**Caveat on evidence strength (added after independent cross-validation):** both samples are small (n=10 each) and were deliberately selected *on the outcome* — i.e., "successful" and "struggling" businesses were chosen because they already showed those outcomes, not sampled at random from the whole market. Treat every count below ("4 of 10", "0 of 10") as **directional evidence of a pattern in this specific sample**, not a statistically representative measurement of the Spanish plumbing market. The research also observed page *structure* only — no click-through, conversion-rate, or analytics data was available for any site, so structural patterns should be read as "what successful businesses tend to do," not "what causes success." This distinction matters most in Sections 2 and 4 below.

## 1. What winners have that losers don't (the core contrast)

| Dimension | Winners (10/10 studied) | Losers (10/10 studied) |
|---|---|---|
| **Identity** | Real business, one domain, consistent across the web | 3 of 10 are multi-city "doorway" template networks (identical page, city name swapped: Servicio Xpress, Servis Instal Company, Repara Home Express) |
| **Named humans** | Family/founder narrative common (years, name of founder) | **0 of 10** show a named, photographed technician or owner |
| **Star rating shown on-site** | 4 of 10 display it directly; the rest have it but don't surface it (a gap even winners often miss) | Self-reported, unverifiable, or absent; one uses "5/5 (2 votos)" as if meaningful |
| **Testimonials** | 5 of 10 show real, named, dated reviews (Instalaciones Piqueras: ~20 full testimonials on-page; Escandic Atlantic & Fonta Teatinos: live embedded Google-review widgets) | Where present, fabricated-looking: stock avatar images filenamed `user-female.webp`, generic names, no link to a real source |
| **Pricing transparency** | Rare even among winners — only 1 of 10 (JF Servicios/Madrid) publishes real starting prices; 3 more use a soft "presupuesto sin compromiso / fixed quote before starting" promise | Either no pricing info, or a bait-and-switch pattern: OCU complaint against Repara Home Express describes a quoted 380€ "desplazamiento" fee "discounted" to 190€ only after the customer balked |
| **Technical health** | All 10 live, functioning, no dead links found | 2 of 10 have a confirmed broken asset (a 404 on the reviews page of "Fontaneros 24 Horas Madrid Urgencias"; a dead DNS entry for "Fontanero Urgente Bcn" — its only web presence is a Yellow Pages listing pointing to a domain that no longer resolves) |
| **Formal complaints on record** | None found | 3 of 10 have a documented OCU consumer complaint, centered on pricing deception or refusal to issue an invoice |
| **Years-in-business as headline** | 5 of 10 put it in the H1/hero itself (Fonta Teatinos' H1 is literally "Fontaneros en Málaga desde hace más de 40 años") | Generic "24H urgencias" framing instead; tenure, if claimed, is unverifiable and generic ("+7 años", "15 años") |

**The single biggest, most fixable gap even among the winners:** most businesses with genuinely excellent Google ratings do not display the number on their own homepage. Cabal has a 4.8★/157-review profile — a asset most competitors, winners included, are failing to surface. Displaying it prominently is not table stakes here; it is a real differentiator.

## 2. The website structure winners actually use (quantified)

Across the 10 successful sites, these sections recur consistently, though their exact ordering varies site to site (the sequence below is the most common arrangement, not a fixed rule; the one clear outlier, Instalaciones Rade, is a thin B2B-leaning site used as a negative reference):

1. **Hero** — headline (often *is* the years-in-business claim) + short subheadline + **first CTA appears before or beside the photo, not after it** (7 of 10 lead with text+CTA, photo second; only 1 of 10 opens with a full-bleed photo-first hero; 2 of 10 use a video hero instead of a photo).
2. *(Often)* a trust stat strip or bullet row — years / guarantee / rating shown as 3–4 tiles (Grupo Dena's is the clearest example: `24h Servicio urgente | 4.9★ Google Reviews | +20 Años experiencia | 100% Garantía`).
3. **Services grid** — present on all 10, no exceptions.
4. **"About us" / "cómo trabajamos" narrative** — the family/process story.
5. **Testimonials / reviews** — when present, always mid-to-lower page, never above the fold; the most complete implementations (Escandic Atlantic, Fonta Teatinos) use a *live, verifiable* widget, not static text.
6. **Certifications / partner-brand logos** — when present, function as institutional trust signals (Instalaciones Piqueras: FEMEVAL, ASEIF, plus "Empresa Autorizada por el Servicio Territorial de Industria y Energía").
7. **FAQ** — only 2 of 10, but the ones that have it (JF Servicios, FONKASA) use it to pre-empt exactly the objections that stall a call ("¿cobráis la visita?", "¿venís hoy?").
8. **Pricing table** — only 1 of 10, but it is a clear differentiator where used.
9. **Contact form / final CTA.**
10. **Footer** — contact details, hours; the "urgencias" operators pad this with a long service-area/neighborhood list for local SEO.

**CTA pattern:** click-to-call is universal (10/10), always phrased as a direct imperative with urgency or no-obligation framing — "Llámanos", "Llama ahora", "Pide/Solicita presupuesto (sin compromiso)" — never a soft "Learn more"-style verb as the primary action. WhatsApp as an explicit *first* CTA is rare (2/10) despite being common in this market as a secondary channel — meaning a strong WhatsApp-first CTA, which fits Cabal's "escríbenos por WhatsApp" brand-manual line, is not yet crowded territory.

**Visual tone in winners:** blue/white "water" palettes dominate; real, unstaged jobsite/team photography appears in half (5/10); the other half lean on flat icon-illustration grids. Orange/warm-accent palettes cluster specifically around businesses using "24h urgente" positioning — this is useful context for Cabal's copper accent, which reads as artisanal/warm rather than alarmist, and should stay visually distinct from that association per the brand manual's anti-red-alarmism rule.

## 3. Website Quality Scoring Matrix

A 100-point rubric across **10 weighted categories**, built directly from what separated the 10 winners from the 10 losers, plus additions flagged by independent review as necessary for a legally- and technically-complete Spanish local-business site even though the 20-site study didn't directly measure them (rows 9–10 below). Use this to score any competitor site — or Cabal's own drafts — objectively.

**Why these weights:** the three highest-weighted categories (Identity, Reputation, Hero/CTA — 15 pts each) map to the three failure modes that most clearly separated winners from losers in the study: anonymity, unverifiable trust claims, and a buried or vague call to action. The remaining categories (10 pts each) are either less differentiating in the sample (testimonials, pricing, content depth — present in a minority of even the winning sites) or weren't directly observed in the study but are non-negotiable baseline requirements for a Spanish local-business site (technical health, legal compliance, bilingual/local-SEO signals).

| # | Category | Weight | 0 pts | Partial credit | Full credit |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Identity & authenticity** | 15 | Doorway-network template, no distinct local identity, no legal/business name shown | Real name shown but generic/templated design | Distinct, real business identity; named owners/founders with photos; single coherent domain (not a city-swapped template) |
| 2 | **Trust signals — reputation** | 15 | No rating, no reviews, or unverifiable "X votos" | Rating claimed but not linked to a checkable source | Real star rating + review count displayed prominently, linked to the actual Google Business Profile (or a live embedded widget) |
| 3 | **Trust signals — testimonials** | 10 | None | Generic 2–3 quote carousel, no verification | Multiple named, dated, specific testimonials or a live verified-review widget |
| 4 | **Hero clarity & first CTA** | 15 | No clear headline or CTA above the fold; CTA is vague ("Learn more") | Headline present but CTA is buried below a long scroll or photo | Headline states the core promise/tenure within 1 sentence; a direct, urgency-appropriate CTA (call/WhatsApp/quote) appears at or before the hero image |
| 5 | **Conversion mechanics** | 15 | Only a contact form, no click-to-call | Click-to-call present but not repeated/sticky on mobile | Click-to-call + WhatsApp both present and prominent; sticky mobile call bar; every CTA states the specific action ("Llamar ahora", not "Enviar") |
| 6 | **Pricing / process transparency** | 10 | No pricing information anywhere, only vague "presupuesto sin compromiso" | Soft promise of a fixed quote before work starts, no numbers | Real indicative pricing or a clearly explained "we quote before we touch anything" process with no bait-and-switch risk |
| 7 | **Technical health & performance** | 10 | Broken links, dead domain, no HTTPS, not mobile-responsive | Functional but slow or incomplete (missing FAQ/service-area info, mediocre load times) | All links live, HTTPS enforced, fully mobile-responsive; concrete performance bar: **Largest Contentful Paint <2.5s, mobile Lighthouse performance score >85** |
| 8 | **Content depth (services/FAQ/process)** | 10 | Services vaguely described, no FAQ, no explanation of process | Services listed but generic; FAQ thin or absent | Concrete, specific service descriptions (own trade language, not generic marketing copy) + FAQ that pre-empts real objections + explicit "how we work" section |
| 9 | **Legal & data compliance** | 5 | No Aviso Legal, no Política de Privacidad, no cookie consent despite collecting data via forms/WhatsApp | Basic legal pages present but generic/templated, unclear data-handling explanation | Aviso Legal, Política de Privacidad and RGPD-compliant cookie consent all present and specific to the business; clear statement of what happens to data submitted via form/WhatsApp |
| 10 | **Local SEO & language signals** | 5 | Single-language only where the market is bilingual; no structured data; inconsistent NAP (name/address/phone) across web listings | One of structured data or bilingual coverage present, not both | schema.org LocalBusiness markup present; NAP consistent across site, Google Business Profile and directories; content genuinely available in both required languages (not just a machine-translated toggle) |

**Scoring bands** (illustrative placement based on the qualitative pattern observed for each business in the study — the 20 sites were not formally scored against this exact rubric, since the rubric was built *from* studying them, so treat these as directional reference points, not computed scores):
- **85–100:** Best-in-class — no site in either sample appeared to clear this bar across every category; the gap is real and winnable.
- **65–84:** Strong — the general profile of most of the 10 winners studied (e.g., Fonta Teatinos, Escandic Atlantic), who combine solid identity and reputation signals with some gaps (pricing, FAQ, or legal/technical detail not directly observed).
- **40–64:** Mediocre/inconsistent — winners with a genuinely strong underlying reputation who nonetheless hide it (e.g., FONKASA, Haquiestoy fail to surface their own rating on-site).
- **Below 40:** Failing — the general profile of the 10 losers studied: anonymous, unverifiable, or actively complaint-generating.

Cabal's target: **85+**, specifically by not repeating the single most common mistake even winners make — hiding a genuinely strong reputation (4.8★/157 reviews) instead of surfacing it — while also clearing the legal/bilingual/technical baseline (rows 7, 9, 10) that the structural study didn't directly test for but that a real Spanish local-business site cannot skip.

## 4. The blueprint: recommended structure for Cabal's homepage

Mapped directly to the brand manual's four message pillars (confianza de toda la vida · trato directo · precio claro · oficio completo) and to the structural patterns evidenced above. (Note: the study observed page structure, not conversion data — "structurally strongest" below means "most complete implementation observed," not "proven to convert better.")

0. **Language — non-negotiable, and not covered by the structural study.** The brand manual explicitly calls for bilingual ES/CA with Catalan-first SEO strings (*"Cabal · Fontaneria i Calefacció a Mataró"*, *"Lampistes des de 1988"*) — Mataró is a Catalan-speaking town. None of the 20 sites studied were assessed for bilingual handling, so this requirement comes directly from the brand manual, not from the competitor research; it should be treated as a hard requirement regardless. Ship both `ca` and `es` versions of every page (not a machine-translated toggle), with `hreflang` tags and a visible language switch, Catalan as the default for organic local traffic.
1. **Hero** — Headline states tenure + trade in one line, per the brand manual's own draft copy: *"El lampista de confianza de Mataró. Desde 1988."* Subheadline: *"Agua y calor, sin sorpresas."* **Two CTAs beside/before the hero image**, not after it: "Llamar ahora" (primary, click-to-call) + "Escribir por WhatsApp" (secondary) — mirroring Escandic Atlantic's dual-CTA structure, the most complete hero implementation found, while filling the WhatsApp-as-primary-CTA gap almost nobody else occupies.
2. **Trust stat strip immediately below the hero** — 4 tiles, styled with the copper accent per the brand manual's 10% rule: `4,8★ · 157 reseñas | Desde 1988 | Familia Galeano & Linares | Mataró y el Maresme`. This single section closes the #1 gap identified in Section 1 — most competitors, including winners, fail to surface a rating this strong.
3. **Los cuatro servicios** (Cabal Agua / Calor / Obra / Comunidades) as a clean 4-tile grid — universal across all 10 winners, non-negotiable.
4. **"Cómo trabajamos"** — a short, numbered process block that makes the precio-claro promise concrete (e.g., 1. Llamas o escribes → 2. Vamos y miramos → 3. Te decimos el precio antes de tocar nada → 4. Lo arreglamos). This directly operationalizes the brand manual's "gran diferenciador" and gives Cabal the fixed-quote-before-work promise that only 3 of 10 winners bother to state.
5. **Reseñas reales de Google** — live-embedded widget or curated real screenshots, named and dated, following the Escandic Atlantic / Fonta Teatinos pattern (the two most complete social-proof implementations found). Per the brand manual, this is explicitly "el mejor material gráfico que hay" — use it, don't bury it.
6. **Zona de servicio** — Mataró + Maresme town list, both for user clarity and for the local-SEO value the footer-list pattern demonstrably provides in this market.
7. **Preguntas frecuentes** — cover exactly the objections the brand manual names (¿cuánto cuesta?, ¿venís hoy?, ¿trabajáis fines de semana?, ¿dais garantía y factura?, ¿reparáis mi marca de caldera?, ¿localizáis fugas sin picar toda la pared?). Only 2 of 10 winners do this well; it is a low-competition, high-value section.
8. **Formulario sencillo** that pre-fills a WhatsApp message — a hybrid no competitor studied implements, combining the form-based lead capture most winners still offer with the WhatsApp-first channel the brand explicitly wants to emphasize.
9. **Footer** — horario, dirección/taller, teléfono, legal info (Aviso Legal, Política de Privacidad, cookie consent — required by RGPD/LOPD once the form or WhatsApp flow collects personal data), schema.org LocalBusiness structured data, and the same trust numbers repeated (rating, "desde 1988").
10. **Mobile:** sticky bottom bar "Llamar / WhatsApp" per the brand manual's own explicit requirement — matches the sticky click-to-call pattern found in two of the competitor sites studied (JF Servicios, DAG Instalacions).

### Explicit differentiation opportunities (gaps no competitor — winner or loser — currently fills)
- **Surfacing the Google rating prominently**, done by only 4 of 10 winners and 0 of 10 losers.
- **A dual call+WhatsApp hero CTA**, done by only 1 of 10 winners.
- **A real, itemized "how we work" pricing-before-we-start process**, done by only 1–3 of 10 winners and actively contradicted by 3 of 10 losers' complaint patterns.
- **Named, photographed owners** — done by zero of the 20 sites studied on either side. For a two-person family business, this is a low-cost, high-differentiation move directly supported by the brand manual's "hablas con Félix o con Ramón" positioning.

### What to avoid (evidenced failure patterns)
- Do not build a template reusable for other cities — 3 of the 10 losers studied are exactly this, and it is detectable within seconds by a visitor (identical phrasing, city name swapped) and correlates with the OCU complaints found.
- Do not let any page go stale or broken — a dead reviews-page link undercut exactly the trust signal one competitor was trying to build.
- Do not use vague "presupuesto sin compromiso" as the *only* pricing story — it is the default across the market and does nothing to differentiate; pair it with the concrete numbered process in Section 4.4.
- Do not use red/alarmist "24H URGENCIA" framing — confirmed as the dominant visual signature of the lower-trust segment of this market, and explicitly ruled out by the brand manual already.

## 6. Independent cross-validation

This document was reviewed by a second, independent model pass acting as a skeptical reviewer with no involvement in the original research, specifically to catch overclaiming and gaps. Its core findings, and how each was handled:

- **Structure ≠ conversion.** The original draft repeatedly described structural patterns (e.g., dual call/WhatsApp CTAs, live review widgets) as "converting" or "conversion-optimized." The study never measured conversion, click-through, or sales data — only page structure. **Fixed:** all such language above now reads "structurally strongest" / "most complete implementation" rather than implying proven conversion lift.
- **Selection bias understated.** Both samples (10 winners, 10 losers) were deliberately chosen because they already displayed the outcome being studied, and n=10 per side is small. **Fixed:** an explicit caveat now opens Section 0, and counts throughout should be read as directional, not statistically representative.
- **Scoring bands implied the rubric was tested on the 20 sites; it wasn't** — the rubric was built *from* studying them, then applied qualitatively after the fact. **Fixed:** Section 3's bands are now explicitly labeled "illustrative placement... not computed scores."
- **Bilingual ES/CA — the single biggest gap identified.** The brand manual requires it explicitly; the original draft never mentioned language at all, meaning a team following the blueprint literally could have shipped a Castilian-only site for a Catalan-speaking market. **Fixed:** added as item 0 in Section 4 and as scoring row 10.
- **Missing baseline requirements not covered by the structural study**: page-speed/Core Web Vitals thresholds, RGPD/LOPD legal compliance, and local-SEO structured data (schema.org, NAP consistency). None of these were things the 20 competitor sites were assessed on in a way that would let a "no context" reader apply them — **fixed** by adding concrete thresholds to the Technical Health row and two new scoring rows (Legal & data compliance; Local SEO & language signals).
- **Section ordering overclaim.** The original text asserted a specific section *sequence* was common to "8–9 of 10" sites, when the underlying evidence showed those sections *exist* on most sites but not that they appear in a fixed order. **Fixed:** softened to "these sections recur... ordering varies."
- **Minor:** an earlier draft mislabeled the scoring matrix as "7 categories" when 8 (now 10) were listed — corrected.

No changes were made to the core, best-supported finding of this research — that most competitors, including successful ones, fail to surface a strong Google rating on their own homepage — since the reviewer confirmed this was the most solidly evidenced claim in the document.
