# Research Report — Pass A: 10 Successful Fontanería/Calefacción Businesses in Spain
Conducted 2026-07-13 via FireCrawl scraping + web search. Referenced by [Website-Blueprint-Cabal.md](Website-Blueprint-Cabal.md).

---

## Method note on evidence quality
Google review ratings/counts were triangulated from web search snippets pointing to Google Business Profile data, cross-checked across 2+ independent third-party listing sites where possible (habitissimo, trustlocal, infontaneros, fontanerodirecto, etc.), since Google Maps itself blocks direct scraping. Where sources disagreed on exact review counts, the range is reported and flagged. Two businesses (Instalaciones Rade, and the DAG Instalacions Google rating specifically) have unverified or low-confidence rating data; they're kept in the set because their websites are real, live, and structurally informative (one is the closest business to Cabal geographically), and each caveat is stated explicitly below.

## Part B — Per-business homepage structure (top to bottom)

### 1. Instalaciones Alejandro Cobano (Valencia)
- **Hero:** Text-first — headline "Fontanero Valencia" + subheadline "Más de 35 años de experiencia a tu servicio" + first CTA "LLÁMANOS" (click-to-call) appears immediately, above the hero photo. A real photo (hand tightening a drain pipe) follows below the CTA.
- **CTAs on page:** click-to-call (repeated 3×), 4× "MÁS DETALLES" internal links, 1 contact form.
- **Trust signals:** years (35) in hero; a mid-page bullet list (Experiencia / Profesionalidad / Confianza / Precios competitivos / Garantía). A "Reseñas" section header exists but no actual review content renders — a real gap.
- **Section order:** Hero → intro/family-business paragraph → 4-tile services grid → trust bullet list → CTA banner → 3 service link tiles w/ photos → real jobsite photo gallery (6 images) → empty "Reseñas" heading → contact form → footer.
- **Pricing:** No numbers; only "presupuesto sin compromiso" in body text.
- **Tone:** Blue/white, real (unstaged) jobsite photography, simple template build.

### 2. Instalaciones Piqueras (Requena, Valencia)
- **Hero:** No large photographic hero — opens straight into 3 service description blocks (Calefacción / Gas / Servicio Técnico), each with a "Más información" link.
- **First CTA:** "¡Quiero contactar!" after a value-prop paragraph mentioning financing and 25 years — mid-page, not in a hero.
- **Trust signals:** the standout of the set — a very long block of ~20 full named, dated customer testimonials embedded directly on the homepage, plus 6 partner-brand logos (Naturgy, Nedgia, Mapfre, Primagas, Cobra, Centro Confort) and 4 association/certification logos (FEMEVAL, ASEIF, ATA, AVACE), plus "Empresa Autorizada por el Servicio Territorial de Industria y Energía de Valencia."
- **Section order:** Top contact bar → 3 service teasers → value-prop paragraph → financing CTA → testimonials block → partner-brand logo wall → 3 detailed service sections → association logos → regulatory-authorization line → contact form.
- **Pricing:** No numbers; "Financiación a tu medida" only.
- **Tone:** Clean, text/authority-heavy, social-proof-first rather than photography-first.

### 3. Grupo Dena (Bilbao/Bizkaia)
- **Hero:** Text-first — "Desatascos urgentes en Bilbao." + subheadline mentioning <60min response, 20+ years, "presupuesto cerrado antes de empezar." Two CTAs immediately: "Pedir Presupuesto" + "📞 Urgencias 24h," both above any photo.
- **Trust-signal stat strip** right after the hero: `24h Servicio urgente | 4.9★ Google Reviews | +20 Años experiencia | 100% Garantía trabajo` — clearest early, above-the-fold, numeric rating display of any site studied.
- **Section order:** Hero + dual CTA → stat strip → reviews-widget placeholder + trust bullet list → real fleet/equipment photo gallery → company description + service-area links → 8-tile services grid → footer.
- **Pricing:** No numbers; explicit "closed quote before starting work" promise.
- **Tone:** Blue, urgency-oriented, real fleet photography.

- **Pricing:** none.
- **Tone:** Minimalist, industrial/B2B, notably colder and less consumer-warm than the other 9 despite claiming "empresa familiar" — the thinnest site of the 10.

## Part C — Cross-business synthesis (patterns, with counts)

**1. Hero structure — text/claim first, photo second, is the dominant pattern.**
7 of 10 lead with headline + years/urgency subheadline + CTA before the photo (Cobano, Dena, Escandic, JF Servicios, Fonta Teatinos, Rade, Piqueras). 2 of 10 use a video hero (DAG, FONKASA). Only 1 of 10 uses a full-bleed photo with text overlaid simultaneously (Haquiestoy).

**2. Click-to-call is universal; the one CTA every winner has.**
10/10 include at least one click-to-call link, usually repeated 2–5×. WhatsApp as an explicit prominent CTA appears in only 2/10 (Escandic, Haquiestoy). "Pedir/Pide/Solicite presupuesto" appears in 5/10. Standalone contact forms on 7/10. Dominant CTA wording: direct imperative + urgency/no-obligation framing — never a soft "Learn more"-style verb as the primary action.

**3. Star rating / review count is under-displayed on-site, even by businesses with excellent ratings.**
Only 4/10 (Dena, Escandic, JF Servicios, Fonta Teatinos) display an actual number on their own homepage. The other 6 — including some with very strong external ratings (Cobano near-5.0, FONKASA 4.9/246, Haquiestoy 4.8–4.9) — do not surface their Google numbers at all. Written testimonials appear on 5/10; the other 5 have none visible. Years-of-experience is in the hero itself on 5/10.

**4. Section order — a repeatable "brochure" sequence emerges across most of the 10 (excluding the thinnest site, Rade):**
Hero (claim + years + first CTA) → trust stat strip/bullets (sometimes) → services grid (10/10, universal) → about/how-we-work narrative → testimonials/reviews (mid-to-lower page, never above fold) → certification/partner logos (when present) → FAQ (2/10) → pricing table (1/10) → contact form → footer.

**5. Pricing transparency is rare.** Only 1/10 (JF Servicios) publishes actual starting prices. 3/10 use "presupuesto sin compromiso/gratis" or a fixed-quote promise as a soft signal. The remaining 6 show no pricing framing at all.

**6. Mobile-specific patterns** were only partially observable via static scraping, but 2 sites show explicit sticky/floating click-to-call elements (JF Servicios, DAG), and `tel:` links appear in the header/hero of all 10.

**7. Visual tone.** Real, unstaged jobsite/team photography used by 5/10 (Cobano, Dena, Escandic, Piqueras, Fonta Teatinos); the rest lean on flat icon-illustration grids or generic stock imagery. Blue/white "water" palettes dominate; orange/warm accents cluster around "24h urgente"-positioned sites. WordPress+Elementor is the visible tech stack behind at least 4/10, and repeated copy patterns suggest several sites were built by the same class of local-SEO agency rather than fully bespoke.

## What this means for Cabal (brief, evidence-linked takeaways)
- Lead with the years-in-business claim in the H1/subheadline (Fonta Teatinos, Cobano, Dena, Haquiestoy, Rade all do this) — supports "el lampista de confianza de toda la vida."
- Put the phone CTA above the photo, not buried below it — 7/10 winners do this.
- Actually show the Google rating number on the homepage — the single biggest, most fixable gap seen across 6/10 successful businesses.
- A real testimonials block (named, specific, several of them) is rare but clearly effective — Piqueras' wall and Escandic/Fonta Teatinos' live embeds are the standout executions.
- Nobody in this set nails pricing transparency except one Madrid operator — a simple "starting from €X" table would be a genuine point of difference from 9/10 businesses studied.
